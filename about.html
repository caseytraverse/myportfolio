Growing up, I was always very fond of both Art and English class. Art was where my visual imagination could run wild on canvas. English was where I could communicate emotion through pen and paper.

/ / /

When I was 12 years old I had spent the better part of that year trying to convince my father that our family needed to join the elite few in town who owned a desktop computer with... internet access!

I had spent a lot of time on Windows Paint before we got our fancy new computer so when I discovered Adobe’s Photoshop, Dreamweaver, Fireworks and Flash - it was as if I had discovered a hidden buried treasure. While other kids were playing video games I was learning HTML and designing websites.

/ / /

Some 15 years later I formed my organic bath and body soap company while living on Maui, Hawaii. I decided to build the company website myself thinking back to my experience over a dozen years prior. “How much could have changed in 15 years?” I asked myself.

Well, a lot - come to find out. Via a lot of trial and error, countless tutorials and google searches I successfully created my companies website.

/ / /

Recognizing how much had evolved, I set out to embrace these new challenges. In 2019 I completed a twenty-month mentorship at Bloc. The apprenticeship focused on UI/UX Design with the foundations of Frontend Development. Creating friendly visual designs that offer real-world solutions was not what I would have thought I was setting out to do all those years ago. But as it turns out - it was.
